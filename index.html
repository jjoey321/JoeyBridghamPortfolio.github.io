Featured Case Study

The Taylor family: a full private wealth plan.

A six-week private wealth engagement (FIN 540, UNCW). The clients: Mark, 65, a newly retired optometrist, and Lisa, 62, a professor retiring within three years. The picture: roughly $3M in investable assets, a $180K annual spending need, a $100K home renovation, 529 funding for three grandchildren, and a donor-advised fund for philanthropy. Every part of the project builds toward one goal: writing and executing an Investment Policy Statement that carries the Taylors through retirement.

Case Brief → Portfolio Holdings →
Part 1: The IPS

Foundations & Time Horizon

Why the IPS is the cornerstone document

Established the IPS as fiduciary communication tool, behavioral discipline, and performance benchmark. Segmented the Taylors' multi-stage time horizon into Lisa's three remaining working years, the gap until Mark claims Social Security at 70, and the long joint retirement beyond, then framed their risk and return objectives around it.

Download Part 1 →
Part 2: Capital Market Expectations

Return, Volatility & Correlation Assumptions

The inputs that drive everything downstream

Built forward-looking return, volatility, and correlation assumptions for the major asset classes using historical data and building-block methodology, cross-checked against institutional forecasts including the J.P. Morgan and AQR 2026 Long-Term Capital Market Assumptions.

Download Part 2 →
Part 3: Asset Allocation

Efficient Frontier & Recommended Mix

Mean-variance optimization, tied back to the IPS

Ran Markowitz mean-variance optimization on the capital market expectations to build an efficient frontier, then selected the allocation the IPS pointed to: roughly two-thirds equities and one-third bonds and real assets, squarely inside the risk tolerance both clients identified.

Download Part 3 →
Part 4: Fund Selection

Implementing Every Sub-Asset Class

Specific, low-cost vehicles for each exposure

Selected specific ETFs and mutual funds for every sub-asset class in the allocation, screened on expense ratio, index tracking, and qualitative factors, building the portfolio from scratch before considering transition costs.

Download Part 4 →
Part 5: Client Communication

The Client Letter

Translating the plan into plain English

Wrote the letter previewing the recommendations and addressing the Taylors' fear of market volatility head on: behavioral coaching grounded in market history, plus a stress test showing the plan survives a one-in-one-hundred-year drawdown while staying inside their stated 25% maximum one-year loss.

Download Part 5 →
Part 6: Implementation

Transition Plan & Asset Location

Final deliverable

The closing phase: transitioning from the Taylors' current holdings to the recommended portfolio while weighing embedded capital gains, and locating each asset class in the right account type (taxable, traditional IRA, or Roth) for maximum after-tax wealth.
